<p class="p1"><span class="s1">Let \(f(x) = k{x^2} + kx\) and \(g(x) = x - 0.8\)</span>. The graphs of \(f\) and \(g\) intersect at two distinct points.</p>
<p class="p1">Find the possible values of \(k\).</p>
</div>
$$$$
<div class='question'>
<p>attempt to set up equation &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(M1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\;\;\;f = g,{\text{ }}k{x^2} + kx = x - 0.8\)</p>
<p>rearranging <strong>their </strong>equation to equal zero &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>M1</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\;\;\;k{x^2} + kx - x + 0.8 = 0,{\text{ }}k{x^2} + x(k - 1) + 0.8 = 0\)</p>
<p>evidence of discriminant (if seen explicitly, not just in quadratic formula) &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(M1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\;\;\;{b^2} - 4ac,{\text{ }}\Delta&nbsp; = {(k - 1)^2} - 4k \times 0.8,{\text{ }}D = 0\)</p>
<p>correct discriminant &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(A1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\;\;\;{(k - 1)^2} - 4k \times 0.8,{\text{ }}{k^2} - 5.2k + 1\)</p>
<p>evidence of correct discriminant greater than zero &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>R1</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\;\;\;{k^2} - 5.2k + 1 &gt; 0,{\text{ }}{(k - 1)^2} - 4k \times 0.8 &gt; 0\), correct answer</p>
<p>both correct values &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(A1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\;\;\;0.2,{\text{ }}5\)</p>
<p>correct answer &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>A2 &nbsp; &nbsp; N3</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\;\;\;k &lt; 0.2,{\text{ }}k \ne 0,{\text{ }}k &gt; 5\)</p>
