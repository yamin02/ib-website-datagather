<p>The heights of adult males in a country are normally distributed with a mean of 180 cm and a standard deviation of \(\sigma {\text{ cm}}\). 17% of these men are shorter than 168 cm. 80% of them have heights between \((192 - h){\text{ cm}}\) and 192 cm.</p>
<p>Find the value of \(h\).</p>

<div class='question'>
<p>finding the \(z\)-value for 0.17 &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(A1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\(z =&nbsp; - 0.95416\)</p>
<p>setting up equation to find \(\sigma \)<em>, &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong>(M1)</strong></em></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\(z = \frac{{168 - 180}}{\sigma },{\text{ }} - 0.954 = \frac{{ - 12}}{\sigma }\)</p>
<p>\(\sigma&nbsp; = 12.5765\) &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(A1)</em></strong></p>
<p><strong>EITHER (Properties of the Normal curve)</strong></p>
<p>correct value (seen anywhere) &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(A1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\({\text{P}}(X &lt; 192) = 0.83,{\text{ P}}(X &gt; 192) = 0.17\)</p>
<p>correct working &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(A1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\({\text{P}}(X &lt; 192 - h) = 0.83 - 0.8,{\text{ P}}(X &lt; 192 - h) = 1 - 0.8 - 0.17,\)</p>
<p>\({\text{P}}(X &gt; 192 - h) = 0.8 + 0.17\)</p>
<p>correct equation in \(h\)</p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\(\frac{{(192 - h) - 180}}{{12.576}} =&nbsp; - 1.88079,{\text{ }}192 - h = 156.346\) &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(A1)</em></strong></p>

<p>\(h = 35.7\) &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>A1 &nbsp; &nbsp; N3</em></strong></p>
<p><strong>OR (Trial and error using different values of <em>h</em>)</strong></p>
<p><strong>two</strong> correct probabilities whose 2 sf will round up <strong>and</strong> down, respectively, to 0.8 &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>A2</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\({\text{P}}(192 - 35.6 &lt; X &lt; 192) = 0.799706,{\text{ P}}(157 &lt; X &lt; 192) = 0.796284,\)</p>
<p>\({\text{P}}(192 - 36 &lt; X &lt; 192) = 0.801824\)</p>
<p>\(h = 35.7\)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;<strong><em>A2</em></strong></p>
