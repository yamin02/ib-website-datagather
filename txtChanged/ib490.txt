
<div class='question'>
<p>In the expansion of \(a{x^3}{(2 + ax)^{11}}\), the coefficient of the term in \({x^5}\) is 11880. Find the value of \(a\).</p>
</div>
$$$$
<div class='question'>
<p>valid approach for expansion (must have correct substitution for parameters, but accept an incorrect value for \(r\)) &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(M1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\(\left( {\begin{array}{*{20}{c}} {11} \\ r \end{array}} \right){(2)^{11 - r}}a{x^r},{\text{ }}\left( {\begin{array}{*{20}{c}} {11} \\ 3 \end{array}} \right){(2)^8}{(ax)^3},{\text{ }}{2^{11}} + \left( {\begin{array}{*{20}{c}} {11} \\ 1 \end{array}} \right){(2)^{10}}{(ax)^1} + \left( {\begin{array}{*{20}{c}} {11} \\ 2 \end{array}} \right){(2)^9}(ax) + \ldots \)</p>
<p>recognizing need to find term in \({x^2}\) in binomial expansion &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(A1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\(r = 2,{\text{ }}{(ax)^2}\)</p>
<p>correct term or coefficient in binomial expansion (may be seen in equation) &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(A1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\(\left( {\begin{array}{*{20}{c}} {11} \\ 2 \end{array}} \right){(ax)^2}{(2)^9},{\text{ }}55({a^2}{x^2})(512),{\text{ }}28160{a^2}\)</p>
<p>setting up equation in \({x^5}\) with <strong>their</strong> coefficient/term (do not accept other powers of \(x\)) &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(M1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\(a{x^3}\left( {\begin{array}{*{20}{c}} {11} \\ 2 \end{array}} \right){(ax)^2}{(2)^9} = 11880{x^5}\)</p>
<p>correct equation &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>(A1)</em></strong></p>
<p><em>eg</em>\(\,\,\,\,\,\)\(28160{a^3} = 11880\)</p>
<p>\(a = \frac{3}{4}\) &nbsp; &nbsp; <strong><em>A1 &nbsp; &nbsp; N3</em></strong></p>
